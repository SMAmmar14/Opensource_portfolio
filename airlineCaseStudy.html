UI/UX Case Study

SastaTicket Building a Better Flight Booking Experience

  • Role Product Designer
  • Duration

    2nd1stMar 2021 - 18thMar 2021

  • Sector

    Travel Technology

  • Practices User Survey, Brain Storming, Sketching Logo Designing

About the project

As part of my UI/UX journey. I wanted some work that people would love to see when they stumble across my portfolio. Therefore I decided to conduct a UX case study about a brand that I am avid user of when it comes to travelingSastaTicket. Therefore I had took on the challenge of improving the user experience of SastaTicket's website.

A little about SastaTicket

SastaTicket has been created to empower the Pakistani traveler with instant bookings and comprehensive choices. It aims to deliver value by offering a range of travel products with the highest standard of service along with cutting edge technology.

So, Why It Keeps the Poor Design?

Because SastaTicket like every other business out there believes that “Customers Drive the Product”. So their website looks that way because customers have told them this the product they want. That's what I think. mmmm... So why does Expedia and Booking.com have a better looking interface? Are there customers personas not the same as SastaTicket's?

Still Not Convinced?

How about redesigning the website of SastaTicket and try to improve the UX as a proof of concept while also considering the current user experience in mind?

The Mission

Improve the website so that it can

  • User can find the best deals on flights and hotels
  • Ability to find the best tourist spots
  • Able to book flights and hotels on a specific date
Design Process
  • Conduct initial screener survey followed by analyzing the current IA and gather data
  • Backup our hypothesis with data
  • Perform Usability Testing by interviewing the Travelholics
  • Redesign the website based on the new analysis
  • Do Solution evaluation and validation
Analysis

We will start the process by listing down the findings from the initial screener survey and then move on to analyzing the current IA.

The first question that comes in the mind was, Why there was a need for a screening survey?
The main reason I setup the survey was to seek out the users who might qualify for user interviews and the aim was to uncover the demographics and travel booking experience. I used google forms for the survey and shared the link among people who I knew liked to travel. Some of the questions asked in the survey are listed below.

  • What is your age?
  • What sort of traveling do you typically do?
  • What is your Gender?
  • How often do you travel?
  • How do you book the flights?
  • What is the most important factor when booking a flight?
  • Rate your overall online booking experience
Screener Survey Findings
  • Around 70% of survey was filled by males.
  • 78.6% people used to travel for just fun and vactional purposes. While the rest for business.
  • So out of 15 people 5 only used aggregator websites for booking tickets. While the rest preferred the dedicated airline websites.
  • Majority of the survey participants used website as their medium for booking tickets.
  • 11 out of 15 particpants who filled the survey felt that price is the most important factor when booking an ticket, followed by the right date and the time.
  • 10 participants were quite happy with the overall booking experience.

To recap, most participants were avid travelers.

Competitive Analysis

After completing the initial screening survey it was important to understand the competitors that SastaTicket currently have. Therefore I decided to research about 5 companies that were operating under the same domain. After searching google, I came up with 4 direct and 1 in-direct competition that SastaTicket I believe have.

The Interviews

I didn’t have access to a usability testing lab, so I just picked 3 of my friends with different backgrounds and mentalities, yet they have one thing in common: they love to travel.

Since I had no access to the usability testing lab. I conducted the sessions over Google Meet and and I would like to thank these people who collaborated with me by taking out some time out of their busy routines.

I compiled things on the Post-its such as likes, things that were hated by participants. Although I would love to note their behavior, but they insisted on not opening the camera and I respected it.

These session helped me create an initial Affinity Map where just jotted down all the things from the session. After spending couple of hours on sorting and selection I was able to come up with an Affinity Map that was more precise and relevant to the cause.

There was one comment that stood out for me which was in Urdu so I wrote in Roman Urdu.

Some of the findings from the interview session were.

  • 2/3 thought there was too much text on the landing page.
  • 1/3 felt like the Deals section was giving a spammy vibe.
  • All of them were of opinions that the filters should be on left hand side.
  • All of them were of the opinion that there should be a seat reservation option while booking the flight
  • 1/3 felt at ease, When he say the trust payment providers at footer.
  • 3/3 observed that the website lacked consistency in its components and elements.
